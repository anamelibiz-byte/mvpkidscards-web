How One Soccer Mom Raised $412 in a Single Weekend — Without Leaving Her Couch

By the MVP Kids Cards Team • June 12, 2025 • 6 min read

Maria had tried GoFundMe, bake sales, and car washes. None of them felt right. Then she created an MVP Kids Card for her daughter Sofia — and everything changed. Here's her story, in her own words (and a few of ours).

Maria V. has three kids in youth sports. Her youngest, Sofia, is nine years old and plays travel soccer for a competitive U10 club team in Austin, Texas. Like most travel soccer families, Maria and her husband spend nearly every weekend of the spring and fall shuttling Sofia to tournaments — and writing checks for registration fees, hotel rooms, matching warm-up gear, and a coach's travel stipend.

"It adds up to several thousand dollars a year," Maria told us. "We love it. Sofia loves it. But I'm always looking for ways to bring that cost down without making her feel like she's a burden."

The Fundraising Grind (And Why Most of It Doesn't Work)

Maria had been through the youth sports fundraising gauntlet. GoFundMe pages that got a few pity donations from grandma and then flatlined. Chocolate bar sales that Maria mostly ended up buying herself. A car wash that raised $63 on a hundred-degree Saturday. A wrapping paper catalog — don't even ask.

"All of it felt like begging," she said. "Either I'm sending people a link and hoping they feel sorry enough to donate, or I'm dragging my kids to a parking lot for four hours to wash strangers' cars. Neither one is fun, and neither one actually works."

"All of it felt like begging. Either I'm sending people a link and hoping they feel sorry enough to donate, or I'm dragging my kids to a parking lot for four hours to wash strangers' cars."

— Maria V., soccer mom, Austin TX

What Maria really wanted was something she could feel proud of — something that represented Sofia, her hard work, her personality, and her story — that other people would actually want to have. Not just give money to. Actually own.

Discovering MVP Kids Cards

A friend in Sofia's soccer club mentioned MVP Kids Cards in the team's group chat. Maria had never heard of it. She clicked the link, read through the site, and within about ten minutes had talked herself into trying it.

The concept was simple: she'd upload a photo of Sofia in her kit, fill in her stats (height, position, GPA, a few favorite things), choose a card edition, and get a run of physical trading cards — professional-quality, the kind that look like they belong in a pack alongside the pros.

Once the cards were designed and printed, she could sell them through the platform's built-in marketplace, or share a direct link and let family and friends purchase directly. Each sale funds Sofia's sports season.

How the economics work: MVP Kids Cards takes a 15% platform fee on marketplace resales. For direct sales through a parent's personal link, the family keeps the full card price minus production and shipping. Maria priced Sofia's standard edition at $18 per card.

Creating Sofia's Card

Maria spent about 45 minutes setting up Sofia's card. She dug through her camera roll to find the right photo — not a posed team portrait, but an action shot from a game, Sofia mid-sprint, ponytail flying, expression fierce and focused. She filled in the stats: 5'1", 63 lbs, midfielder, GPA 3.9, favorite pre-game meal (chicken nuggets — Sofia insisted), and a short bio she and Sofia wrote together at the kitchen table.

"That was honestly one of my favorite parts," Maria said. "Sofia got so into it. She was like, 'Mom, make sure you put that I've been playing since I was four. And that I want to play in college.' She's nine! But she means it."

She chose the Standard Edition — clean design, gold foil accents, serial-numbered out of 25. She ordered 30 cards. The total production cost came to $67.

The Weekend It All Happened

Maria shared the card link on a Friday evening — just a text to her family group chat and a post in the soccer club's parents Facebook group. She wrote a short note explaining what it was, what Sofia was saving up for (a fall tournament in Dallas), and a link to purchase.

By Saturday morning, 12 cards had sold. By Sunday night, she had sold 27 out of 30.

"I literally didn't do anything except send that one text and one Facebook post," Maria said. "I didn't remind anyone. I didn't follow up. People just bought them."

The most surprising buyers? Not just family. Three of Sofia's coaches bought cards. Two parents from opposing teams who had seen Sofia play reached out asking if they could get one. A coworker of Maria's husband — someone who had never met Sofia — bought two.

Why It Worked (And Why Other Fundraisers Don't)

When we asked Maria why she thought this worked when GoFundMe and bake sales hadn't, she didn't hesitate: "Because people got something. They're not donating — they're buying. Sofia's card is sitting on my brother-in-law's desk right now. He's proud of it. He shows it to people."

That's the key insight behind MVP Kids Cards, and it's one that traditional youth sports fundraising almost always misses. Donation fatigue is real. People are constantly being asked to give to causes, campaigns, and GoFundMe pages. But buying something — especially something with perceived lasting value, something connected to a real person they care about — feels completely different.

  • Tangible value: Buyers own a physical collectible. It's not a donation receipt — it's something they keep, display, and show off.
  • Emotional connection: The card tells the athlete's story. Grandma doesn't just donate $18 — she buys a piece of Sofia's story that she can hold in her hands.
  • Novelty: Nobody has been asked to buy a youth athlete trading card before. It's new, it's fun, and it generates genuine curiosity.
  • Serial numbering: The fact that each card is numbered out of 25 (or whatever edition you choose) creates a sense of scarcity and collectibility. People feel like they're getting something exclusive.

What Happened Next

Sofia's fall tournament in Dallas? Fully funded. Maria has already started designing a second card — a Foil Edition — for Sofia's spring season. She's also been the loudest voice in her club group chat recommending MVP Kids Cards to other families.

"I told everyone: don't do another car wash. Do this instead." So far, four other families in Sofia's club have created cards.

And Sofia? She carries one of her own cards in her soccer bag. She pulls it out occasionally to show teammates. She is, by all accounts, extremely proud.

"Sofia carries one of her own cards in her soccer bag. She pulls it out to show her teammates. She's nine years old and she has a trading card. That's just the coolest thing."

— Maria V.

Ready to Try It Yourself?

If you've got a young athlete with a big season ahead, MVP Kids Cards might be the last fundraiser you ever have to run. It takes about an hour to set up, it's something your athlete will be proud of, and — based on Maria's experience — it actually works.
