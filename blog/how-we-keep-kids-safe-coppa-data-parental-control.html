🔒 Safety & Privacy

How We Keep Kids Safe: Our Approach to COPPA, Data, and Parental Control

By the MVP Kids Cards Team • April 20, 2025 • 7 min read

We built MVP Kids Cards with one non-negotiable: parents are always in control. No child ever creates an account. No child's contact information is ever collected. No data is sold. Ever. Here's exactly how we protect your child's data and likeness — in plain English.

When we were designing MVP Kids Cards, we talked to a lot of parents about what they worried about when it came to their kids and the internet. The answers were remarkably consistent: they worried about who could see their child's photo, who owned their child's data, and what happened to that information over time.

These are the right questions to ask. And we built our entire platform to answer them with complete transparency.

The Fundamental Design Choice: Kids Don't Have Accounts

The most important privacy decision we made is also the simplest: children never have accounts on MVP Kids Cards. Full stop.

When you create a card for your athlete, you — the parent or guardian — create the account. You upload the photo. You enter the stats. You manage the sales. You control who can purchase, and you receive all proceeds. Your child is the subject of the card, not a user of the platform.

This is a deliberate inversion of how most youth-facing apps work. Instead of building a platform that children use (and then trying to layer privacy protections on top), we built a platform that parents use to celebrate and fundraise for their children. The child's role is to be featured — not to interact with the software.

Children never have accounts on MVP Kids Cards. The parent is always the account holder, decision-maker, and data controller.

What Is COPPA and Why Does It Matter?

COPPA — the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act — is a federal law (15 U.S.C. §§ 6501–6506) that regulates how websites and online services collect personal information from children under 13. It requires verifiable parental consent before any personal information from a child under 13 is collected, used, or shared.

Many apps in the youth sports space handle COPPA compliance by adding a consent checkbox to a sign-up form. We think that's insufficient. Our approach is structural: because children never create accounts and we never directly collect personal information from children, the most sensitive COPPA obligations simply don't apply to us in the same way.

The information on a child's card is entered by their parent, on a parent's account, under a parent's control. Parents can view, modify, or request deletion of all data at any time. For a full description of our COPPA approach, see our dedicated COPPA Compliance page.

Who Can See Your Child's Card?

Your child's card is only visible to people you share it with. By default, an athlete's card page is not indexed by search engines, not discoverable through our platform's browse or search features, and not shared with any third party. The only way to reach a card page is to have the specific link that you, as the parent, choose to share.

Photo Rights and Athlete Likeness

Your child's photo belongs to you. You upload it, you retain ownership, and you grant MVP Kids Cards a limited license to use it for the sole purpose of producing the card you ordered. We do not use athlete photos in our marketing, advertising, or any other context without explicit written consent from the parent or guardian.

When you place an order, you confirm that you have the legal right to use the photo and likeness in question — typically because you are the parent or legal guardian of the athlete, and you took the photo (or own the rights to it). We cannot accept photos of athletes for which you do not have appropriate authorization.

A note on AI and image use: We never use athlete photos to train AI models, and we never share photos with any third party except our print production partner, who uses them solely to print the cards you ordered.

Payment Processing and Financial Security

All payments on MVP Kids Cards are processed by Stripe, one of the world's most trusted payment processors. We never see, store, or handle your full credit card number — it goes directly to Stripe's PCI-DSS compliant servers and never touches ours. Stripe handles all card authorization, fraud detection, and payment security.

Card proceeds are held in escrow by our payment processor and disbursed to the parent account holder on a regular schedule. We do not have discretionary access to your funds.

Our Data Promise in Plain English

  • We do not sell your data or your child's data to anyone, ever.
  • We do not share athlete information with advertisers.
  • We do not send marketing to children.
  • We do not use your child's photo for any purpose other than printing their card.
  • We do not allow other users to search for or discover your child's card without your link.
  • You can request deletion of all data at any time by emailing us.

Have a specific question about privacy or data? We welcome it. Email us at hello@mvpkidscards.com or read our full Privacy Policy and COPPA Compliance page. We believe transparency builds trust, and we're happy to explain anything.

Why This Matters to Us

We're parents too. The people who built MVP Kids Cards have kids in youth sports. We know what it means to share a photo of your child online and wonder who's going to see it. We built the platform we wished existed — one where the answer to "who can see my kid's photo?" is simply: "only the people you choose to show it to."

We think protecting children online isn't a compliance checkbox. It's the foundation everything else is built on. And we're committed to earning your trust, one card at a time.
