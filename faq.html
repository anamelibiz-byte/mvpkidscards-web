Teams & Fundraising

Running a team campaign is simple:

  • Step 1: The organizer (coach, parent rep, or PTA coordinator) sets up a team campaign in minutes
  • Step 2: Each player/participant gets their own unique card — sport, dance, academic, or any other category
  • Step 3: Families receive their shareable card link to send to grandparents, aunts, uncles, and family friends
  • Step 4: Supporters purchase cards, and the fundraising profit goes to your team or program
  • Step 5: Cards ship directly to your team organizer for distribution at a team event
Real example: A 15-player travel soccer team each sells 20 cards → that's 300 cards × $12 net per card = $3,600 raised in one campaign.

It depends on your team size and how many cards each family sells, but here are some realistic benchmarks:

  • Large team or school class (30 participants, 25 cards each) → ~$9,000 raised

Cards are a high-motivation fundraiser because every family is selling a card with their own child on it — not generic candy or gift wrap.

Blockchain & Ownership

When a card is minted on-chain, a permanent, tamper-proof record is created on the blockchain — like a digital birth certificate for the card. This record contains:

  • The athlete or participant's name
  • The card edition (e.g., Rookie Edition, Gold Edition)
  • The serial number (e.g., #1 of 25)
  • The date the card was created

This record cannot be altered or deleted — which means a card's authenticity can always be verified, even decades later.

Read our full blockchain explainer for parents →

No. We take privacy very seriously, especially for minors. The blockchain record contains only non-sensitive card metadata:

  • ✅ First name only (or display name of your choice)
  • ✅ Card edition and serial number
  • ✅ Creation date
  • ❌ No photo
  • ❌ No last name (unless you choose to include it)
  • ❌ No contact information
  • ❌ No home address or school name

Photos and full personal information are stored on our secure, encrypted servers — never on the public blockchain.

MVP Kids Cards are not NFTs in the speculative cryptocurrency sense. We use blockchain technology purely as a provenance and authenticity tool — the same way a certificate of authenticity works for a signed jersey.

We don't ask you to set up a crypto wallet, buy cryptocurrency, or think about gas fees. Blockchain is working quietly in the background to protect the authenticity and collectible value of your child's card.
