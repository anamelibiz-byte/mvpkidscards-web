🔍 Feature Deep-Dive: What Snapshot Does Really Well

1. Teams Page — Full Self-Service Flow (/teams)

This is Snapshot's most powerful conversion page. A coach or parent fills out one form and the entire team workflow kicks off. Key fields:

  • Team name, sport (16 options), location (state), player count
  • Contact info (name, email, phone — they text updates)
  • Cards needed by deadline (auto-sets internal deadline 7 days prior)
  • "Who pays?" toggle — Players Pay ($29.99/player) or Team Pays (upfront bulk)
  • Template selection — "let players choose" or lock to one design
  • Ships to coach for distribution

MVP Opportunity: Replicate this exact flow but add a Fundraising Goal field — how much does your team need to raise? Show the math live: "15 players × 25 cards × $18 = $6,750 potential." Make fundraising the hero, not an afterthought.

2. Organizations Page — Commission Partner Program (/organizations)

Snapshot offers leagues/schools up to 15% commission on all sales through a partner form. Checkboxes they offer orgs:

  • Custom Team Templates (org-branded)
  • Bulk Card Orders
  • Custom Booster Boxes
  • Team Fundraising
  • MEGA Cards (oversized display prints)
  • Snapshot LIVE Contests
  • Custom Branding

MVP Opportunity: Turn this into a full League & School Partner portal — not just a form. Give organizations their own dashboard with revenue tracking, team management, and a fundraising calculator. This is MVP's biggest gap and biggest opportunity.

3. FAQ Page — 13 Questions in 4 Categories (/faq)

Snapshot's FAQ covers: Getting Started (3Q), Pricing & Payment (3Q), Printing & Quality (3Q), Shipping & Delivery (3Q), Teams & Organizations (3Q). Key answers MVP needs to match:

  • Free to create + preview; only pay to print
  • JPG, PNG, HEIC photo formats; 300 DPI minimum
  • Cards $17.99 each; team discounts based on boxes ordered
  • Ships worldwide; US 3–7 days; international 7–14 days
  • Tracking via email + dashboard
  • Team billing is invoiced after order confirmation

4. Resources Hub — SEO Content Engine (/resources)

Snapshot's resources page is a massive SEO play — it's a content hub with links to guides targeting every possible search query around custom cards. Key resource categories:

  • Custom Cards by Sport: baseball, football, basketball, soccer, lacrosse
  • Card Types: holographic, trading cards, playing cards, mega/oversized
  • How-To Guides: photo tips, sizing guide, design guide
  • Team Guides: team sets, booster boxes, team ordering
  • Gift Ideas: cards as gifts, special events, keepsakes

MVP Opportunity: Build a Resources hub focused on the fundraising angle — "How to run a card fundraiser," "How much can your team raise?", "Fundraising vs. other options," etc. MVP has the blog for individual posts; a hub page ties it all together for SEO.

5. Template Gallery — Visual Card Picker (/templates)

A visual grid of all card styles with free/NEW badges. Snapshot's current templates (10+ and growing):

  • Skyline (AI city background), Shattered (glass effect), Urban Rise (graffiti), BOOM! (color explosion)
  • Legacy (heritage/retro), Paint Blaster (paint burst), Modern Chrome (foil/metal), Afterdark (neon)
  • Neon Bomb (pop-art), Stencil (spray paint), + more in pipeline

🏆 Where MVP Kids Cards Beats Snapshot

These are features Snapshot doesn't have at all. They are MVP's competitive moat — lean into them hard.

  • Dedicated Fundraising Engine — Snapshot mentions fundraising once in a checkbox. MVP makes it the entire reason to exist. Team fundraising pages, campaign math calculators, progress tracking, shareable fundraising links.
  • Blockchain Provenance / On-Chain Cards — Snapshot is 100% print. MVP cards have verifiable digital ownership records — a collectibles story Snapshot can't tell.
  • Secondary Marketplace — MVP cards can be resold. Snapshot cards are paper. This changes the value proposition entirely — parents invest, not just spend.
  • COPPA Compliance Page — Snapshot has nothing. MVP's explicit COPPA page builds trust with schools and youth orgs that have data governance requirements.
  • Transparent Refund Policy — Snapshot has no public refund page. MVP's clear refund policy reduces purchase hesitation.
  • Athlete Profile Pages — MVP has /athlete-profile.html. Snapshot has no concept of a persistent athlete identity — just one-off orders.
