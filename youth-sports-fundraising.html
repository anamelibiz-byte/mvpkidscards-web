The Problem with Traditional Youth Sports Fundraising

Every youth sports season brings the same routine: a packet of coupon books, a box of chocolate bars, or a sign-up sheet for a car wash. Parents dread it. Kids dread it. And the results are usually underwhelming — low margins, awkward asks, and products nobody actually wants.

Youth sports programs across the country spend enormous time and energy on fundraisers that generate thin returns. A typical chocolate bar fundraiser returns 40–50% to the team after product costs. A restaurant night might raise $200 if you're lucky. Meanwhile, parents have to do all the legwork — driving, collecting cash, tracking orders — with no digital infrastructure to help.

MVP Kids Cards changes the model entirely. Instead of asking families to sell an unrelated product, you give them something they actually want: a professional trading card of their own athlete. The card is the product and the fundraiser in one.

How MVP Kids Cards Fundraising Works

1

Coach or Admin Sets Up a Team Account

Takes about 2 minutes. Choose your revenue model — how proceeds are split between your team pool and individual families. No upfront cost.

2

Share One Team Code with Families

Every family gets your team's join code. They sign up at mvpkidscards.com/join, create their athlete's card, and are automatically connected to your team fundraiser.

3

Families Share Their Card Links

Each family gets a personal card page they share via text, email, or social. Grandparents, aunts, uncles, and family friends order directly — no cash, no awkward asks.

4

Revenue Splits Automatically

When a card sells, the revenue automatically splits according to your model: a portion to your team fundraising pool, a portion to the individual family. No manual tracking.

5

Track Everything in Your Dashboard

Real-time dashboard shows total raised, top sellers, goal progress, and payout status. Connect your bank via Stripe for automatic payouts.

Revenue Models

Every team is different. That's why we offer four flexible revenue models so you can structure fundraising however makes sense for your group.

🏆

Team 20% / Family 80%

The most popular split. For every $29.99 Foil card sold, $6 goes to your team pool and $24 goes to the family. Motivates maximum participation.

🎚️

Custom Split

Set the exact percentage — anywhere from 5% to 50% to your team. Tune it to your group's goals and what works for your parent community.

💰

Team Keeps 100%

All card revenue goes to your team fundraiser. Great for a specific drive — new uniforms, tournament travel, or equipment upgrades.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Keeps 100%

Your team earns nothing — families keep all proceeds for their individual athlete. Great for leagues that just want to offer the service with no fundraising pressure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it cost anything to set up a team fundraiser?

No — setting up a team account and launching your fundraiser is completely free. You only pay when cards are purchased, and the revenue share model is taken from sale proceeds automatically.

How do payouts work?

Team fundraising proceeds are paid out via Stripe. You connect your bank account from your dashboard, and payouts are processed on a regular schedule as sales come in.

Can families participate if they don't live nearby?

Yes — that's one of the biggest advantages. Because everything is online, grandparents and family in other states can buy cards and cards ship directly to them. No one has to be local.

What if only some families participate?

Even partial participation beats most traditional fundraisers. A team where 10 out of 20 families each sell 3 cards at a 20% split can net $360+ with zero upfront investment.
